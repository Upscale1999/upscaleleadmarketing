HOW IT WORKS

A system built for private dental practices.

Six moving parts, all designed around the way UK patients actually choose a private dentist.

1

Treatment-specific Meta & Google ads

Separate campaigns per treatment, geo-fenced to your catchment, with creative built around the actual treatment outcomes (within GDC and CAP code limits, no outcome guarantees).

2

Treatment landing pages

One page per treatment, with finance options, before-after gallery, practice accreditations (CQC, GDC), team specialisms and a treatment-specific consultation booking form.

3

Pre-consultation qualification

Form captures treatment interest, urgency, finance vs. cash, and patient preferences. The clinical team gets a one-line summary so consultations open from a position of context, not a cold start.

4

Speed-to-lead automation

SMS confirmation within seconds, email with practice details and finance brochure, plus a self-service booking link to slot into the next available consultation. Most practices still take 24 to 48 hours to call back, you'll respond in seconds.

5

No-show reduction sequence

24-hour and 1-hour SMS reminders, plus a re-engage flow for missed slots. Typical practices drop no-show rates from 30 to 40 percent down to 10 to 15 percent within the first month.

6

End-to-end consultation reporting

You see ad spend, leads generated, consultations booked, consultations attended, and treatment value signed. Plain English, no jargon, no 40-tab dashboard your practice manager will never open.

FAQ

Dentists ask us this a lot.

The questions we hear most often on a first call with a practice owner or principal.

What dental treatments do your campaigns target?

High-ticket private treatments: Invisalign and clear aligners, dental implants and All-on-4, composite and porcelain veneers, smile makeovers, cosmetic bonding, teeth whitening packages, full-mouth restorations and dental phobia / sedation. We can do NHS work if needed, but the maths usually only works for private treatments where the case value is above £1,500.

Are your campaigns GDC and CAP-code compliant?

Yes. Every ad and landing page is built around the GDC Standards (specifically guidance on advertising) and the CAP code rules on cosmetic treatment marketing. No before-after images of registered medicines (Botox), no comparative claims, no testimonials presented as outcome guarantees, no targeting of under-18s for cosmetic treatments.

How much does a high-ticket dental lead cost?

Invisalign leads typically run £40 to £100 per qualified consultation booked. Implant leads £70 to £180 (case value justifies it). Veneers and smile makeovers £60 to £150. We optimise to cost-per-consultation-attended, not cost-per-form-fill, because no-shows are the silent killer of dental ad ROI.

How do you handle no-shows for consultations?

Three things. One, the booking form asks qualifying questions (treatment interest, urgency, budget range) so consultations are pre-qualified. Two, the system sends automated 24-hour and 1-hour reminders by SMS and email. Three, we provide weekly no-show rate reporting so we can spot drift and address it. Typical dental no-show rates drop from 30 to 40 percent down to 10 to 15 percent.

Can you target by specific postcode catchment?

Yes. Most dental practices set a 10 to 20 mile catchment radius from the practice. We tightly geo-fence ads to that area, ignoring the rest of the UK entirely. For practices with multiple sites, each gets its own radius and campaign so leads route to the right location automatically.

How quickly can you get a dental campaign live?

Seven days from kick-off. We build the landing page (one per high-ticket treatment), set up tracking on your existing booking system or our own, design the SMS / email follow-up flow, and go live. Most practices see their first consultation booked within 48 hours of ads going live.

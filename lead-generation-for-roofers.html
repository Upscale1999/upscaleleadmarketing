THE PROBLEM

Why most roofer marketing doesn't pay for itself.

If you've spent money on Checkatrade, MyBuilder, Yell or generic Google ads and walked away frustrated, you're not alone. Here's what tends to go wrong.

Shared leads, racing to the bottom

The big lead boards sell the same enquiry to three or four roofers in your area. You're competing on price the moment you call, and the homeowner already has two other quotes lined up.

Tyre-kickers and £50 jobs

Generic ad campaigns attract anyone with a leaking gutter. Without proper qualification, your team wastes half their week driving out to estimate jobs that were never going to be profitable.

Slow follow-up kills good leads

UK research shows roofing leads are 5x more likely to convert if contacted within 5 minutes. Most roofers are on the roof when an enquiry lands, and by the time they call back, the homeowner has booked someone else.

No tracking, no learning

Most roofing campaigns have no link between an ad click and a booked job. So you can't tell which ad, audience, or landing page actually drove the £6k roof replacement, which means you can't double down.

Seasonal swings handled badly

Demand spikes after storms. Most agencies run fixed budgets that miss the spikes and overspend in the quiet months. You need a system that flexes with the weather.

Generic landing pages

The ad sends them to your homepage. They don't see your accreditations, recent work or pricing approach. Half of them bounce, the other half submit a generic "contact us" with no detail you can act on.

HOW IT WORKS

The system that turns clicks into booked roof jobs.

Five moving parts, all built specifically for the way UK roofing businesses actually win work.

1

Targeted Meta & Google ads

Geo-fenced to your service area. Audiences modelled on the type of homeowner who actually books roof work (older properties, owner-occupied, postcodes with the right house values).

2

Roofing-specific landing pages

Built around your accreditations (NFRC, CompetentRoofer, Guild of Master Craftsmen), real photos of your previous work, and an enquiry form designed to capture the size and type of job.

3

Built-in lead qualification

Every enquiry filters for job type (whole roof / repair / flat / fascia), property type, urgency and rough timescale. Tyre-kickers are screened out before they reach your phone.

4

Seconds-fast follow-up automation

The instant a homeowner submits, they get an SMS thanking them and a link to book a survey slot. You get a notification with all the detail. No more racing to call back from a ladder.

5

Weekly performance reviews

You see cost-per-lead, cost-per-job-won, which ad creatives drove the biggest tickets, and where to scale. Plain English, no jargon, no 60-tab dashboard.

6

Demand-flex ad budget

Spend scales up automatically when storm forecasts come through or seasonal demand spikes, and pulls back in quiet months. You get more roof jobs at the right time, and waste less in the slow season.

FAQ

Roofers ask us this a lot.

The questions we get most often on a first call with a roofing company.

What kinds of roofing jobs do your leads turn into?

Mostly whole-roof replacements, large repair jobs, flat-roof installs, fascia & soffit work, and insurance-related work after storm damage. We can also generate repair-only leads if that's your model, but most clients prefer to filter for higher-ticket work because that's where the margin is.

Are these shared leads like Checkatrade or HomeAdvisor?

No. Every lead we generate is yours and only yours. We don't sell the same enquiry to three or four competing roofers like the big lead resellers, because that race-to-the-bottom is exactly why most roofers hate paying for leads.

How much does a roofing lead typically cost?

UK roofing leads typically come in at £30 to £100 each depending on the job type, location and time of year. Whole-roof and large repair leads sit at the higher end, simple gutter or repair leads at the lower end. We optimise to the lead-to-job-won ratio, not the lead price, because a £80 lead that closes a £6,000 job is cheaper than a £20 lead that goes nowhere.

What about seasonal demand and storm spikes?

Roofing demand is genuinely seasonal, with big spikes around autumn and winter storms. We scale ad spend up and down with the weather and the market so you're not paying for cold leads in mid-summer or missing the gold rush after a major storm event. The system flexes with your capacity.

How quickly do you follow up with leads?

Within seconds. The moment a homeowner submits the form they get an SMS confirming their enquiry, an email with your details, and a slot to choose for a callback. Speed-to-lead is the single biggest factor in whether a roofing lead actually books, and we automate it so your team isn't tied to their phone.

What service area can you cover?

Anywhere in the UK or Ireland. We can tightly geo-fence ads to specific postcodes, a defined drive-time radius from your depot, or specific towns and cities, whichever fits your service area best. If you only cover a 30-mile radius, we only pay to advertise to people in that 30-mile radius.
